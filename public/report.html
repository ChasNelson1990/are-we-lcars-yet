NX-01 2151 · ENT

NX-01 runs on manual switches and dials with no touchscreen bridge interface. Its defining trait, repeatedly and on screen, is a universal translator that doesn't work — which is why Hoshi Sato is a main character. No holodeck exists anywhere aboard; the only holographic technology the crew encounters is explicitly alien.

USS Discovery 2256–2258 · DISCO S1–2

Pre-Zora Discovery: a standard Starfleet computer plus the mycelial network, a navigation medium for the spore drive — not a data-storage system, despite how it's sometimes described. The ship briefly has holo-communicators; a malfunction gets them stripped out fleet-wide, which is the direct, on-screen reason Strange New Worlds has none.

Catalogued requests

Request Episode Outcome Notes

Real-time Klingon translation via communicator Disco 1×09 "Into the Forest I Go" Learns in real time UT actively acquires Klingon rather than translating instantly; S1 also leaves long stretches subtitled, untranslated.
Contain the Control AI's attempted takeover of Section 31's network Disco season 2 arc ("Control") Contained after crisis A hostile outside AI, not this ship's own computer becoming sentient — a distinct entity from Zora.

Real World 2026 · Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini

This tier isn't dramatized canon, so it isn't subject to the same episode-citation methodology as the other nine — it reflects publicly documented capabilities of current frontier assistants rather than a script. It wasn't independently re-researched this pass; the comparison page's own footnote already flags its more speculative claims (genuine AI sentience remains "unresolved and undemonstrated, not just roleplayed around").

Method & sources

  • Primary sources: the chakoteya.net full-episode transcript archive (covers ENT, TOS, TNG, DS9, VOY) and Memory Alpha, the Star Trek wiki, cross-checked against episode recaps for Discovery/Picard/SNW, which chakoteya.net doesn't cover.
  • Coverage is uneven by design: NX-01, TOS, TNG, and VOY each got a deep research pass (7–12 verified instances). Discovery (both eras), SNW, DS9, and Picard got thinner passes (1–3 instances each) — marked per section rather than padded to look even.
  • Two technical snags came up during research and were resolved: a WebSearch session rate limit (affecting ENT and TOS, resolved on retry) and a chakoteya.net TLS certificate handshake failure (worked around by fetching over plain HTTP).
  • "Time taken" is reported only when an episode states or clearly depicts a duration on screen; most requests genuinely have no stated time — that's an accurate reflection of the source material, not a data gap.
  • This is not an exhaustive census of every computer interaction across nine series — it's a citation-backed sample chosen for variety and verifiability, not completeness. A larger, systematic transcript-processing pass is planned as a follow-on project and will backfill the thinner sections above.
  • Verdict definitions: Supported a specific scene or line backs the claim · Partially supported the general idea holds but a detail is off, unattributed, or overstated · Not supported contradicted by, or absent from, the source material · Can't verify plausible but no citable on-screen source found.
  • Some Memory Alpha citations above link to the article's standard, predictable wiki address for a page the research explicitly named; not every individual link was re-verified live at publication time.
